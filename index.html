About Me

I'm a third-year Electrical and Electronics Engineering student at Kyrgyz-Turkish Manas University in Bishkek. Right now, I'm on exchange at Ege University in Izmir, Turkey, where I've been lucky enough to work in a robotics lab alongside my coordinator. It's been eye-opening ‚Äî finally getting hands-on experience with the full engineering process, from initial sketches to watching the actual prototype move. What really drives me is the chance to work on something that doesn't exist yet. I love that moment when you're troubleshooting a circuit at 2 AM and suddenly everything clicks. Or when you redesign something three times and the fourth attempt finally works. My plan is pretty straightforward: finish my bachelor's, do a Master's to really specialize (because let's be honest, four years isn't enough to master anything), and eventually pursue a PhD. Not because I want more degrees on paper, but because I want to be the person who figures things out and then teaches others how to do it too. That's the long-term goal ‚Äî research and teaching. For now, I'm just focused on learning as much as I can. Every project teaches me something new, even when it fails (especially when it fails, actually). I'm still figuring out exactly where my path will lead, but I know it involves building things, solving problems, and probably a lot of trial and error.
